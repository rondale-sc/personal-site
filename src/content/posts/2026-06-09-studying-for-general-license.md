The General class license is the second tier of amateur radio licensing in the US — a meaningful step up from Technician. Where Technician mostly opens up VHF and UHF (local repeaters, line-of-sight), General unlocks substantial HF privileges: the shortwave bands where signals travel thousands of miles by bouncing off the ionosphere. That means real DX contacts, chasing rare callsigns, working split frequencies, and participating in the kind of radio I've been most curious about since getting licensed.

The exam is 35 questions drawn from a pool of around 450, covering FCC rules and regulations, operating practices, radio theory, and electronics fundamentals. Passing is 74% — 26 out of 35.

![Studying Subelement G1 — Commission's Rules on HamStudy](/interests/radio/hamstudy-general.png)

Using [HamStudy](https://hamstudy.org) to work through the question pool subelement by subelement. It's a well-made tool — tracks what you're getting consistently right and keeps surfacing the questions that need more attention.

Only been at this a few days, but I'm feeling good. The rules sections are straightforward, and the electronics theory is familiar territory from the 6502 build. Taking the exam next Thursday, June 16th. Expect to walk out licensed.
